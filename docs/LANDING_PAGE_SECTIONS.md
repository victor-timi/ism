# ISM Landing Page — Section Blueprint

The landing page must achieve three things in order:
1. **Hook** — Emotional pull, identity, belonging
2. **Clarify** — What ISM actually does and who it's for
3. **Convert** — Drive sign-ups, hub exploration, community engagement

---

## Section 1: Hero (existing)

**Purpose:** Emotional hook + brand statement

- "JOIN THE MOVEMENT" headline
- "For Students. By Students." tagline
- Brief description of what ISM offers
- Two CTAs: primary (Explore the Hub) + secondary (Learn More)
- Animated stats strip showing international student data
- Growth chart visualization

**Notes:** This section is strong. See review notes for potential improvements around making the value proposition more immediately clear.

---

## Section 2: What We Do / Value Proposition

**Purpose:** Bridge the emotional hero with concrete value. First-time visitors need to understand *what this platform does* within 5 seconds of scrolling past the hero.

**Layout:** Three columns (or stacked on mobile), one for each pillar:

| Pillar | Icon | Headline | Description |
|--------|------|----------|-------------|
| Jobs | Briefcase | Part-Time Jobs | Browse curated casual and part-time work from SEEK, Indeed & more — filtered for student schedules. |
| Housing | Home | Share Accommodation | Find verified flatmate and housing listings aggregated from Flatmates.com.au, Gumtree & university portals. |
| Discounts | Ticket | Student Discounts | Never miss a deal — verified student savings from OzBargain, Finder, and more, ranked by popularity. |

Each card should link directly to the Hub tab.

**Design notes:**
- Clean, minimal cards with subtle icons
- Light background to contrast with the hero
- Consider a brief heading above: "Everything you need. One place." or similar

---

## Section 3: How It Works

**Purpose:** Reduce friction. Show how simple it is to use ISM.

**Layout:** 3-step horizontal flow (numbered):

1. **Browse** — Explore jobs, housing, and discounts in one place
2. **Save** — Bookmark listings and set alerts for new matches
3. **Connect** — Join the community forum and share experiences

**Design notes:**
- Numbered steps with connecting lines or arrows
- Minimal illustration or icons for each step
- This section should feel effortless — no login required to browse (lower the barrier)

---

## Section 4: Featured Listings / Live Preview

**Purpose:** Show the product in action. Social proof through real content.

**Layout:** A small preview grid showing 2-3 real (or realistic) listings from each category — jobs, housing, discounts. This gives visitors a taste of what's inside the Hub before signing up.

**Content:**
- 2-3 job cards with title, location, type (casual/part-time)
- 2-3 housing cards with location, price, room type
- 2-3 discount cards with deal title, source, expiry

**Design notes:**
- Tab switcher at the top (Jobs | Housing | Discounts) or a mixed masonry grid
- "View all" CTA linking to the Hub
- Cards should match the actual Hub card design for consistency
- This is the most important conversion section — it proves the platform has value

---

## Section 5: Community / Forum Teaser

**Purpose:** Highlight the community aspect. ISM isn't just a tool — it's a community.

**Layout:** Split section — left side has messaging, right side has a preview/screenshot of the forum or testimonial cards.

**Content:**
- Heading: "You're not alone in this" or "833,000 students. One community."
- Brief text about the Discourse forum, peer support, shared experiences
- CTA: "Join the Community"

**Design notes:**
- Warmer tone — this is about people, not features
- Could include testimonial quotes from students (real or representative)
- Consider showing forum topic previews or member count

---

## Section 6: Stats / Social Proof (alternative placement)

**Purpose:** If the hero stats feel too data-heavy for the top, this section can live lower as a credibility booster.

**Note:** The current hero already has a strong stats strip. This section is optional — only include if you move the stats out of the hero or want to expand on them with more context (e.g., source citations, year-over-year growth narrative).

---

## Section 7: Call to Action / Sign-Up Banner

**Purpose:** Final conversion push before the footer.

**Layout:** Full-width banner with strong CTA.

**Content:**
- Heading: "Ready to make student life easier?" or "Join 833,000+ international students"
- Subtext: Brief one-liner about ISM's mission
- CTA: "Sign Up Free" (primary) + "Explore the Hub" (secondary)

**Design notes:**
- Accent background (ISM green) or contrasting dark section
- This should feel conclusive — the last push before footer
- Keep it clean and focused — one heading, one line, two buttons

---

## Section 8: Footer (existing)

**Purpose:** Navigation, legal, social links.

- 4-column grid: ISM info, Browse, Company, Legal
- Social media links
- Copyright

---

## Recommended Section Order

```
1. Hero (emotional hook + stats)
2. Value Proposition (what ISM does — 3 pillars)
3. How It Works (3 steps)
4. Featured Listings (live preview of the Hub)
5. Community Teaser (forum + belonging)
6. CTA Banner (final conversion push)
7. Footer
```

## Design Principles

- **Mobile-first**: Every section must work beautifully on phone screens
- **Scroll-driven reveals**: Use subtle fade-up animations as sections enter viewport
- **Consistent spacing**: Use the same vertical rhythm between all sections
- **Performance**: No heavy images above the fold. Lazy-load anything below section 2
- **Accessibility**: All text must meet WCAG AA contrast. Interactive elements need focus states
- **Typography hierarchy**: Use the established ISM type scale (text-display, text-h1, text-h2, text-body-lg)
